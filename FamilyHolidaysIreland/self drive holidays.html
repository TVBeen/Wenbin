Self-Drive Tour Holidays

Our Irish self-drive programs enable you to mix-and match so that you can create a perfect Ireland driving holiday. Plan your own itinerary by choosing the ever popular B & B vouchers, either on an open basis or pre-booked. Also choose from a wide selection of deluxe Irish castle hotels, elegant manor houses, cosy inns or modern properties throughout Ireland. We use Hertz Car Rentals exclusively featuring current model vehicles with either manual or automatic gearbox (at a supplement).

Choosing The Ideal Tour

Some people like to wander at will, exploring new places every day. Some enjoy a trip without no worries about making hotel reservations as they travel. And then other people like a trip where they do not need to drive. It is up to you. However, if this is your first visit to Ireland; do consider taking a coach tour as an introduction to the country before heading out on your own. Explore all the possibilities of an Ireland Coach Tour for your next adventure...

Go-As-You-Please With B & Bs

Stay in a real Irish family home to experience a friendly welcome from homeowners. You have the freedom to select from over 1,000 properties and you can choose from a variety of different town, country and farm locations. When you book this trip, we give you open vouchers so that you have complete freedom. However, you may then book your chosen homes in advance through the Bed & Breakfast Ireland website. Experience a real Irish living with a B & B...

Chauffeur-Drive Package

Select a chauffeur-drive tailor-made holiday to fit your requirements exactly, whether for a party of two or a small group. This is a great way for family or friends to explore Ireland and celebrate a special occasion. Choose from our wide range of hotels, make reservations for any dates you wish and your driver will look after you every step of the way. He will reveal all the best places to shop, eat or find entertainment and ensure that you have a truly memorable travel experience. Get your own chauffeur to drive you all over Ireland...
